Ana Stavarache | Construction Activity and Housing Affordability in NYC

This project explores the relationship between housing affordability and construction activity in New York City from 2016 to 2025, with a focus on identifying potential indicators of gentrification. Using 2015 ACS census tract data, I calculated a custom Social Vulnerability Index (SVI) based on the percentage of housing cost relative to income. Higher percentages indicate higher economic stress and housing burden. The spatial data was cleaned, joined, and clipped to NYC’s five boroughs for focused analysis. I then imported and filtered NYC Housing Database records, plotting the latitude and longitude points for new construction, demolitions, and renovations during the 2016–2025 period. By overlaying these construction activity points onto the SVI base map, this project visually examines where development is occurring in relation to economic vulnerability. The analysis raises questions about the spatial logic of redevelopment and its correlation with affordability, revealing patterns that suggest gentrification pressures in certain high-SVI neighborhoods. This work highlights the importance of spatial equity in housing and urban planning decisions.

Marietta Pattison | Exploring the Relationship Between Funded versus Criminalized Art in Charlotte, North Carolina

In a rapidly growing city, such as Charlotte, North Carolina, gentrification can be seen in many areas. In Charlotte, these areas include Uptown and the expanding areas such as South End, NoDa, and Midtown. Art plays a major role in how desirable a neighborhood can be. Graffiti is looked at as being a low form of art, often conducive with the idea that an area is unsafe. Funded art, such as murals, however, aestheticize a place and make for tourist photo-ops. I have conducted a comparison analysis between city-funded art versus graffiti complaints throughout the city in order to track gentrification throughout Charlotte and comment on this unfair notion that public art should be criminalized in some areas but not others.
